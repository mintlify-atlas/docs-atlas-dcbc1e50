The tone and style you select can transform the same content into completely different speeches. SpeechWriter AI gives you control over how your speech sounds and feels, ensuring it matches both your personality and the occasion.

## Understanding Tone and Style

**Tone** refers to the attitude and emotion conveyed in your speech—whether it's formal, casual, inspirational, or humorous.

**Style** refers to the structure, language complexity, and delivery approach—whether it's conversational, professional, eloquent, or straightforward.

Together, tone and style shape how your audience experiences and remembers your speech.

## Available Styles

<Accordion title="Professional">
Clear, organized, and business-appropriate language. Uses industry terminology when relevant and maintains a polished delivery. Best for business presentations and professional gatherings.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Conversational">
Natural, easy-to-follow language that feels like a genuine conversation. Avoids overly complex sentences and connects directly with the audience. Perfect for weddings and casual events.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Eloquent">
Refined, sophisticated language with carefully crafted phrases and literary elements. Creates memorable moments through powerful word choice. Ideal for keynote addresses and important celebrations.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Straightforward">
Direct, concise language that gets to the point quickly. Minimizes flowery language and focuses on clear communication. Great for business updates and informational speeches.
</Accordion>

## How to Specify Tone and Style

When generating your speech, you'll have the opportunity to select your preferred tone and style:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Consider Your Occasion">
    Think about the event type, venue, and audience expectations. A corporate board meeting requires different tone and style than a backyard wedding.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Reflect Your Personality">
    Choose a tone and style that feels authentic to you. If you're naturally humorous, a casual and light approach may work best.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Match Audience Expectations">
    Consider what your audience expects and what will resonate with them. Industry professionals expect different delivery than family members.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select During Generation">
    Use the tone and style options when inputting your speech details. You can adjust these if the first result doesn't feel quite right.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Impact on Your Speech

Tone and style dramatically affect how your message is received:

### Same Content, Different Tones

**Formal Tone:**
"We are gathered here today to celebrate the union of two remarkable individuals who have demonstrated exceptional commitment to one another."

**Casual Tone:**
"I'm so excited we're all here to celebrate these two amazing people who are clearly perfect for each other!"

**Inspirational Tone:**
"Today we witness the beautiful beginning of a shared journey, a testament to the power of love and partnership."

<Info>
The same key points will be included regardless of tone and style—what changes is how they're expressed and the emotional resonance they create.
</Info>

## Choosing the Right Combination

Here are recommended tone and style combinations for common speech types:

| Speech Type | Recommended Tone | Recommended Style |
|-------------|------------------|-------------------|
| Wedding Toast | Heartfelt or Casual | Conversational |
| Business Presentation | Formal | Professional or Straightforward |
| Keynote Address | Inspirational | Eloquent or Professional |
| Eulogy | Respectful or Heartfelt | Eloquent or Conversational |
| Academic Speech | Formal | Professional |
| Retirement Speech | Heartfelt or Humorous | Conversational |

<Tip>
You can always regenerate your speech with different tone and style settings if the first version doesn't feel quite right. Experiment to find what works best!
</Tip>

<Warning>
Be mindful of cultural context. What's considered appropriate tone and style can vary significantly across different cultures and communities.
</Warning>
